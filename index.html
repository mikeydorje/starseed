The Basics

Starseed is an audio-reactive particle visualizer. Upload a track or use your mic — visuals respond in real time.

Format & Framing

Renders in 16:9, 1:1, or 9:16. ⏣ Aperture Scale adjusts field of view. Boundary makes visuals edge-aware. Experiment with rotation — a saved video at 16:9 rotated 90° becomes a different 9:16; even a rotated 1:1 creates a new composition.

Controls

Eight sliders per scene. Names like Epoch, Flux, and Tremolo hint at function. Some ride slow drift cycles that reveal themselves over time. Randomize to explore. Settings are saved between visits.

Narrative Arc

Each scene evolves visuals over the track length — shorter audio means a tighter, more dynamic arc. Trim to your target length before uploading for best results — 60 seconds for a Reel gives a more dynamic render than cutting a full song after.

Recording

⏺︎ Record renders offline at 60 fps to MP4 via WebCodecs. No server — everything stays in your browser. Keep the tab in foreground and device awake while rendering. On iOS, video won't include audio — screen-record or sync later. Drift cycles may render at different phases than the preview, but settings control the same math.

Live

Listen mode uses your mic or line input in real time — the browser handles device selection. Plug into a projector for live audio-reactive visuals at a show. No record button — use screen record. More coming.

Platform

Desktop recommended — tested on Windows/Chrome, MacOS/Chrome, iOS/Safari.

How it works

Starseed is an audio-reactive visualizer — every particle on screen moves in direct response to your music. Drop in any audio file, and the Web Audio API splits the signal into 64 frequency bands in real time. Those bands push, pull, and reshape a cloud of particles driven by custom GPU shaders every frame. Everything runs on your device's GPU — modern hardware handles this easily, but low-power devices may drop frames.

Eight sliders per scene let you tune the physics: spread, drift, colour, threshold, and more. Aspect ratio, aperture scale, and boundary settings can dynamically alter the visuals in unique ways. A narrative arc slowly evolves the parameters over the length of the track so the visuals tell a story.

Behind the scenes, slow drift cycles — long sine waves between 1 and 5 minutes — continually shift rotation and scale. Combined with the arc, this means no two plays look the same.

Built with ♡︎, Three.js, WebGL, and zero build tools — plain JavaScript and GPU shader math. No AI, no machine learning, no server-side processing. Just code, trigonometry, and your music on static files. Developed on Windows Subsystem for Linux.

Your audio never leaves your device. Files are decoded in the browser and cached in local storage — nothing is uploaded anywhere. For the smoothest experience, keep local browser storage enabled (cookies/local site data) so Starseed can remember your uploaded audio and saved settings.
