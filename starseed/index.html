How it works

Each scene is a cloud of particles driven by custom GPU shaders. Drop in an audio file and the Web Audio API splits the signal into 64 frequency bands in real time — those bands push, pull, and reshape the particles every frame. Modern hardware handles this easily; older machines or low-power devices may stutter or run warm.

Eight sliders per scene let you tune the physics: spread, drift, colour, threshold, and more. Aspect ratio, aperture scale, and boundary settings can dynamically alter the visuals in unique ways. A narrative arc slowly evolves the parameters over the length of the track so the visuals tell a story.

Behind the scenes, slow drift cycles — long sine waves between 1 and 5 minutes — continually shift rotation and scale. Combined with the arc, this means no two plays look the same.

Built with ♡︎, Three.js, WebGL, and zero build tools — plain JavaScript and GPU shader math. No AI, no machine learning, no server-side processing. Just code, trigonometry, and your music on static files. Developed on Windows Subsystem for Linux.

Quick Notes

Built for desktop, primarily tested on Windows/Chrome, MacOS/Chrome and iOS/Safari. Mobile works — fun with Listen mode — but for the full experience (recording, popout controls, format previews), a desktop browser is the way to go.

On iOS, rendered videos won't include audio due to platform restrictions. Use your device's screen recording instead, or hop on a desktop to render with sound.

Hit record and the app renders offline at 60 fps using WebCodecs, muxing straight to MP4 — no server, no upload, everything stays in your browser. Listen mode skips the file and reacts directly to your microphone.

The eight control sliders per scene are intentionally abstract — names like Epoch, Flux, and Tremolo hint at their function. Some parameters ride slow time-based drift cycles and reveal themselves gradually. Move things around, hit Randomize, and let the scene surprise you.

Privacy

Your audio never leaves your device. Files are decoded in the browser and cached in local storage for convenience — nothing is uploaded anywhere. For the smoothest experience, keep local browser storage enabled (cookies/local site data) so Starseed can remember your uploaded audio and dismissed tips.

Version History

Actively evolving — new scenes, features, and refinements ongoing.

v1.0.0 (Beta) — April 2026

18 scenes with control sliders, a narrative arc that shifts the visuals over the length of the track, and background drift cycles.

Playback from file upload or Listen mode (live mic input). Offline recording at 60 fps, rendered and muxed to MP4 entirely in the browser. Format preview overlays for 9:16, 1:1, and 4:5. Uploaded audio is cached locally between sessions.

Popout control panel for adjusting sliders during playback. Randomize button. Fullscreen toggle. Back-to-grid navigation.
